IXP-137 CORE — External Specification

This document describes what the IXP-137 CORE is for, what you can do with it, how it connects to large and small cycles, and how the public interface is structured. Internal engine mechanics remain intentionally undisclosed.

The IXP-137 CORE is a research instrument. Its outputs have no official status, must not be interpreted as real-world predictions, and may not be used for early warning, evacuation decisions, or any safety-critical decisions.

1. Purpose of the IXP-137 CORE

The IXP-137 CORE is an autonomous, crash-safe engine built to explore how a complex field of signals behaves when driven by macro-cycles, micro-cycles, and harmonic interactions. Its purpose is not to forecast reality, but to provide a structured environment to observe:

  • How long-wave envelopes and short-wave fluctuations interact
  • How directional stress-vectors shift under changing cycle conditions
  • How probability envelopes respond to cyclic and quasi-cyclic regimes
  • How an engine behaves under continuous 24/7 research-grade monitoring

2. What You Can Do With It

2.1 Research & Exploration

You can run the CORE in repeated cycles and study:

  • Stability of probabilistic outputs over time
  • Transitions between regimes and bands
  • Emergence of patterns across many cycles

2.2 Cycles, Rhythms & Envelopes

Internally, the CORE links together large and small cycles. Externally, you see the surface behaviour of that coupling in:

  • Shifts in directional stress (N / NE / ... / NW)
  • Changes in event probability and swarm likelihood
  • Variations in regime and band labels
  • Compression or expansion of the active time window

2.3 Logging & Replay

Every cycle writes a full log (stdout + stderr) to disk. This allows:

  • Cycle-by-cycle replay and annotation
  • Offline analysis of probability sequences
  • Correlation studies with external synthetic data
